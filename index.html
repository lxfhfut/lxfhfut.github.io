About

I am a Research Fellow in the School of Information Technology at Deakin University, Australia. Before joining Deakin, I received the Ph.D degree in Computer Science from University of Warwick in 2017 and spent two years as an Associate Lecturer in Computing at Charlese Sturt University, Australia.


My research interests include Digital Forensics, Cyber Security, Data Science and Machine Learning. In the past few years, my research mainly involved the following research tasks of digital forensics via device fingerprint (i.e. unique and reliable pattern/signal left by digital cameras in images) analysis:

1) Source Camera Identification, which is to identify the source camera that has been used to capture the image in question.


2) Large-scale source-oriented Image Clustering. This task involves grouping images coming from the same device in large-scale image databases. This technology is particularly useful for discovering hidden information on Internet, which can be used for different purposes. For instance, we have developed an efficient image clustering algorithm for analysing Interpol’s Child Sexual Exploitation (ICSE) database. The clustering results will be helpful for finding out the possible victims of child sexual abuse on Internet.


3) Image Forgery Detection. This task is to expose the image forgery by detecting the inconsistency between the device fingerprint information left the image in question and the fingerprint of the claimed source camera.
